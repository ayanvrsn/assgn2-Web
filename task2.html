Thomas Edison

Thomas Alva Edison (February 11, 1847 – October 18, 1931) was an American inventor and businessman.

He developed many devices in fields such as electric power generation, mass communication, sound recording, and motion pictures.

Key Contributions
  • Electric Light Bulb – Perfected the design to make it commercially practical
  • Phonograph – The first device to record and reproduce sound.
  • Motion Pictures – Developed cameras and projectors that laid the foundation for cinema.
  • Electric Power System – Built the first power station, bringing electricity to homes and businesses.

Awards
  1. In 1878, Edison was awarded an honorary PhD from Union College
  2. In 1923, the American Institute of Electrical Engineers created the Edison Medal and he was its first recipient.
  3. In 1927, he was granted membership in the National Academy of Sciences.
  4. In 1927, he was elected to the American Philosophical Society.
  5. On May 29, 1928, Edison received the Congressional Gold Medal.
